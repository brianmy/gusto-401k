The $70K vs $7K gap
A business owner who has set up a 401(k) can contribute as both employee ($23,500 limit) and employer (up to $46,500 additional). Combined limit: $70,000. IRA limit: $7,000. In a 32% bracket, the difference in annual tax deferral is $20,160. Most owners without a 401(k) don't know this is available to them. Gusto does — because the salary is in payroll.

Why the projection compounds
SECURE 2.0 tax credits apply in years 1-3 — they offset up to $5,000 of your plan setup costs per year, plus $500 for auto-enrollment. The AUM balance grows at an assumed 7% annual return, compounding the future value every year. This projection would be impossible to personalize without Gusto's payroll data: the contribution amount, the credit eligibility, and the tax bracket all come from the payroll record.
