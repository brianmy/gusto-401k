Several states now mandate that businesses with employees enroll in a state retirement program — or set up their own qualified plan. The good news: Gusto can help you comply in minutes, and you may qualify for up to $16,500 in federal tax credits to cover setup costs under SECURE 2.0.

What this means for your business
If your state has passed a retirement mandate and you don't yet offer a qualified plan, you may face penalties. Offering a 401(k) through Gusto satisfies the requirement and keeps you fully compliant.
Up to $16,500 in tax credits — already calculated for you
Under SECURE 2.0, eligible small businesses can claim up to $5,000/year for 3 years in startup credits, plus an additional $500/year for auto-enrollment. Gusto shows your estimated credit before you commit.
Set up in minutes — payroll data already pre-filled
Because your payroll runs through Gusto, employee info, compensation, and contribution schedules sync automatically. No spreadsheets, no manual entry. Most plans are active within a few days.
Employees auto-enrolled — they can opt out anytime
Gusto's default 401(k) sets auto-enrollment at 3% of pay, which research shows dramatically increases participation. Employees get full control to adjust or opt out from their own dashboard.
